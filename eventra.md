# Problem Statement

## Why Eventra Exists

Communities across East Africa organize thousands of events every day.

These include:

- Weddings
- Fundraisers
- Church conferences
- Community meetings
- Business networking events
- Motor shows
- Car meets
- Bike rides
- Sporting competitions
- Music festivals
- Cultural celebrations
- Educational seminars
- Alumni reunions
- Charity events

Despite the diversity and frequency of these gatherings, there is no widely adopted platform specifically designed around the realities of event organization within the region.

---

# Existing Approaches

Most communities currently coordinate events using general-purpose communication platforms such as WhatsApp.

While WhatsApp is excellent for communication, it was never designed to manage the complete event lifecycle.

Organizers often struggle with:

- Guest management
- RSVP tracking
- Event discovery
- Vendor selection
- Attendance visibility
- Event promotion
- Post-event engagement

Important event information becomes scattered across long conversations and multiple groups.

---

# Discovery Problem

Many events never reach the people who would actually attend them.

People frequently discover events:

- After registration has closed.
- Through forwarded posters.
- By word of mouth.
- Through unrelated social media posts.

There is no centralized platform where communities can easily discover events based on:

- Location
- Interests
- Categories
- Date
- Popularity

---

# Marketplace Gap

Planning an event often requires coordinating multiple service providers.

Examples include:

- Photographers
- Videographers
- DJs
- MCs
- Caterers
- Tent suppliers
- Event decorators
- Sound engineers
- Security providers
- Transport services

Today this process is largely informal.

Recommendations are often obtained through friends or WhatsApp groups.

There is little transparency regarding:

- Previous work
- Customer reviews
- Reliability
- Availability
- Pricing

Choosing service providers therefore becomes difficult and highly dependent on personal networks.

---

# Community Engagement

Most event platforms focus primarily on publishing events.

However, events are social experiences.

People want to:

- See who is attending.
- Share photos.
- Vote during competitions.
- Engage with organizers.
- Receive updates.
- Discuss upcoming activities.

These interactions usually happen across several disconnected applications.

---

# Local Market Opportunity

Global event platforms often focus on conferences, professional networking, or technology communities.

East African communities have different event ecosystems.

They organize events around:

- Families
- Churches
- Schools
- Villages
- Sports
- Motorsports
- Businesses
- Local organizations
- Cultural groups

These communities deserve workflows designed around their realities rather than adapting to products built for different markets.

---

# Beyond Event Discovery

Eventra is not intended to be simply another event listing application.

The vision is to build an ecosystem supporting the entire event journey.

That includes:

- Discovering events
- Registering attendance
- Managing invitations
- Finding trusted service providers
- Receiving updates
- Community engagement
- Event reviews
- Organizer profiles
- Vendor marketplaces
- Post-event memories

The platform aims to connect organizers, attendees, businesses, and service providers within one unified experience.

---

# User-Centered Design

The inspiration for Eventra comes from observing how communities already organize events today.

Rather than replacing existing communication tools, Eventra seeks to complement them by providing features specifically designed for event planning, discovery, coordination, and participation.

Every planned feature is intended to reduce friction, increase trust, and strengthen community participation.

---

# Design Philosophy

Eventra begins with people—not technology.

Its purpose is to make discovering, organizing, and experiencing events simpler, more transparent, and more engaging for communities across East Africa while remaining flexible enough to grow into other regions with similar needs.

Technology is simply the medium through which this vision is delivered.
